Customer clustering for CDC

Task: Try to explore the current status of our bank's existing credit card (CDC) customers from three perspectives: revenue contribution, expected risk and activity.

Variable definition

val: Average monthly contributed revenue
el: Monthly average (on-balance sheet) expected loss
utilization: Monthly average limit utilization

Revenue breakdown of credit card business:

  • Commission (36%): This typically refers to merchant fees (interchange fees) paid by businesses when customers use credit cards. It is usually the largest source of income for credit card companies.
  • Installment fee (28%): Many credit cards offer installment plans for large purchases, where users pay a fee to split payments over time.
  • Interest (25%): Revenue from interest charged on unpaid balances when cardholders donâ€™t pay in full. This is another major revenue stream.
  • Overdue fine (6.5%): Late payment penalties and fees when users miss their due date.
  • Other (4.5%): May include things like annual fees, cash advance fees, foreign transaction fees, etc.

From the perspective of revenue breakdown, commission, interest and installment fee are the main sources of our bank's credit card income. The sum of the three is used as the characteristic variable to measure the revenue contribution of customers (val), that is, the approximate value of the monthly average revenue contribution. The terms EAD, PD, and LGD are key concepts in banking risk management and credit risk modeling, primarily used in the Basel Accords under the Internal Ratings-Based (IRB) framework. These metrics are crucial for calculating the capital requirements for banks. EAD (Exposure at Default): The total amount of exposure (loan balance or credit) at the time of default. PD (Probability of Default): The likelihood that the borrower will default within a specific time frame (usually one year), expressed as a probability value (between 0 and 1). LGD (Loss Given Default): The percentage of the exposure that the bank is expected to lose in the event of default, after considering any recoveries. EL (Expected Loss) is a key metric in credit risk management that represents the average loss a bank expects to incur on a loan or credit exposure over a certain period, typically one year. It is an important concept for determining the capital that a bank needs to hold to cover potential credit losses.
