Factoring

Pricing theory

How to value financial assets after classification is a very complex task, which requires developing clear valuation methods for different financial instruments based on the specific situation of the enterprise and the requirements of IFRS 9. Valuation has a direct impact on the overall profit and revenue of the financial statements, so valuation management is crucial. The valuation of financial instruments traded in the open market is the most convenient and transparent, but there are many financial instruments in China that are difficult to find a pricing market, especially the valuation of non-standard assets. Factoring belongs to this type of product, which has a relatively complex contract structure and its valuation is also relatively complex.

Factoring products are a comprehensive financial service designed for enterprises that sell on credit. It is a financial business that provides financing and other related services for enterprises by acquiring accounts receivable. Normally, the seller of goods (supplier) sells accounts receivable to the factoring company for the buyer of goods, and requires the factoring company (bank) to provide single or multiple services related to this, including accounts receivable collection, sales ledger management, and bad debt guarantee.

The main feature of factoring products is the cash flow paid by buyers of goods at a series of future time points. In the factoring product agreement, the buyer of the goods agrees to pay interest several times in the future and repay the corresponding principal. The interest for each period is calculated based on the remaining principal of the current period and the fixed/floating interest rate specified in the contract. If the valuation date falls between two interest payment dates, accrued interest needs to be subtracted from the present value of future cash flows when calculating fair value. The value including accrued interest is called the dirty price , while the value excluding accrued interest is called the clean price.

Taking standard factoring products as an example, if the interest payment method is a fixed interest rate, the present value of cash flows on the valuation date is:

DP=N∗DF(T0,Tm)+N∗K∗∑i{δ(Tpi−1,Tpi)∗DF(T0,Tpi)}CP=DP−N∗K∗δ(Tpi−1,T0)

If the interest payment method is a floating interest rate, the present value of cash flows on the valuation date is:

DP=N∗DF(T0,Tm)+N∗∑i{δ(Tpi−1,Tpi)∗(F(Tpi−1,Tpi)+s)∗DF(T0,Tpi)}CP=DP−N∗(R(Tpi−1,T0)+s)∗δ(Tpi−1,T0)

In practical business, the elements included in the contract for factoring products are very complex. When discounting, we need to calculate the implied spread. We were also troubled by irregular pricing frequency and irregular interest payment frequency, and even face the possibility of early repayment. All of this makes the study of pricing very interesting. Next, I will make a further exploration for these issues.

Discount curve and implied spread

The overall principle of factoring valuation is discounted cash flow, so the choice of discount curve is crucial. Due to the product characteristics of factoring products, credit risk needs to be considered during valuation. If a guarantee bank guarantees the buyer of goods, then the credit risk comes from the guarantee bank; otherwise, it comes from the buyer of goods. Therefore, the spot yield curve of China Bond related ratings is used as the discount rate curve. Which curve is used depends on the credit rating of the risk subject.

Normally, the discount curve cannot be used directly, it must be adjusted for implied spread. We know that the credit spread reflects the risk level relative to treasury bond, which has been reflected in the yield curve of the corresponding rating. The other part is the implied interest spread of non-standard assets themselves, which is the interest spread actually executed by banks based on their own liquidity and product characteristics, and is a type of interest spread hidden in contracts. The function of implied spread is to calibrate the inaccuracy of ratings.

The method to determine the implied spread is to add it as a variable to the discount rate, calculate the fair value on the effective date, and use the identity that the fair value on the effective date is equal to the nominal principal to solve for the implied spread.

We interpolate the spot yield curve of China Bond on the effective date (as valuation date here), and obtain the spot yield SY(T0,Tpi) from effective date T0 to interest payment date Tpi. Represent implied spread with IS, the formula for the discount factor is:

DF(T0,Tpi)=1/(1+SY(T0,Tpi)+IS)δ(T0,Tpi)

It should be noted that the accrued interest on the effective date is 0, so the clean price is equal to the dirty price. And they are all equal to the initial nominal principal.

CP=DP=N

By combining the above two equations with the pricing formula, the implied spread can be calculated. The implied spread will be used as a constant in fair value calculations on other valuation dates.

Case of floating rate

In the case of floating interest payment, the reference rate for the floating interest rate should be stipulated in the contract. We will construct the corresponding forward interest rate curve based on the market data of the reference rate, and obtain the forward interest rate for the required annualized term through interpolation method. We also need to adjust it according to contract factors such as interest rate multiplier, interest rate spread, or upper and lower limits for floating interest rate.
