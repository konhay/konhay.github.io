Bond

Bond yield

Spot yield

Also known as the current yield, it is the ratio between the annual interest income of a bond and the price paid to purchase the bond. The calculation formula is:

Spot yield = Annual interest income ÷ Purchase price × 100%

For example, if you purchase a bond with a face value of 1,000 yuan and an annual interest of 50 yuan, the spot yield can be calculated by the following formula:

Spot yield = 50 ÷ 1,000 × 100% = 5%

This formula only considers the interest income of the bond, without considering the difference between the selling price and the buying price, that is, without considering the gains or losses of the bond investment, so it can only be used as a reference indicator for investment decisions, and cannot fully reflect the returns of bond investments.

Yield to maturity (YTM)

It refers to the rate of return that can be obtained by purchasing a bond at a specific price and holding it until maturity. It is the discount rate that can make the present value of future cash flows equal to the purchase price of the bond. It takes into account the gains or losses of bond investments and is a more comprehensive yield.

For bonds with installment interest payments,

YTM = (face value × coupon rate × residual maturity + face value - purchase price) ÷ (purchase price × residual maturity) × 100%,

that is, y = (F*c*n+F-P)/(P*n) @Simple interest

For bonds with one-time principal and interest payments,

YTM = (face value × (1 + coupon rate × bond effective life) - purchase price) ÷ (purchase price × residual maturity) × 100%,

that is, y = (F*(1+c*n)-P)/(P*n) @Simple interest

For discount bonds, i.e. zero-coupon bonds,

YTM = (face value - purchase price) ÷ (purchase price × residual maturity) × 100%,

that is, y = (F-P)/(P*n) @Simple interest

In general, the yield to maturity takes into account more factors and is more comprehensive than the spot yield.
